Holistic Admissions

Components of Holistic Admissions

  • Standardized Testing

    • SAT, ACT, SATII Subject Tests

  • Transcript

    • What courses were available to you
    What courses did you take advantage of
    How well did you do in those courses
    How well did you do when finishing high school

  • Letters of Recommendation

    • Choosing teachers who will advocate the strongest and most honestly about your performance and standing in the school and class

  • Extracurricular Activities

    • What did you do outside of school (ex. sports, outside clubs, school organizations, volunteering, research, part-time job, familial responsibilities, commute, etc.)?

  • Essays

    • If you were to drop your essay on the cafeteria floor and it did not have your name on it, would someone pick it up and know that you were the one that wrote it?

  • Interview

    • Be courteous. Be on time. Do your research. Brainstorm questions.

What Does 'Holistic Admissions' Really Mean?

The term 'holistic admissions' means that there is no one part of your application that guarantees you will be admitted and there is no one part of your application that guarantees you will not be admitted. The admissions committee will look at every part of your application to inform their decision. They are not only looking for students who check boxes, but ones who will bring perspective and contribute to their campus in a meaningful way.

Why Do I Use 'A Hill' Analogy?

Everyone will start somewhere on a hill when it comes to college admissions. Some students will have a steeper journey than others depending on their experiences or personal situations. For anyone interested in highly selective admissions, the journey will be difficult as every institution has unique priorities when creating its next class.

How Do You Use This Knowledge Going Forward?

Try out things that you're actually interested in. If you curate your high school experience to fit into a certain college mold, it will not be as fruitfull of an experience.
